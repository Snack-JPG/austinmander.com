## üéØ Core Positioning (Start Here)

### The Foundation Questions
1. **The One-Liner**: How do you introduce yourself at a conference in 10 seconds?
2. **The Transformation**: What specific transformation do you enable for organizations?
3. **The Unique Angle**: What can you do that a McKinsey team can't? (Hint: Your PM tool proves something here)
4. **The Anti-Position**: What do you NOT do? Who do you NOT serve?

### Your Superpower
- What did building your PM tool teach you that consultants who've never built enterprise software don't know?
- What patterns did you discover about why projects fail that your tool specifically addresses?
- What's the #1 insight from your tool's data/usage that would shock executives?

## üé™ Target Market Definition

### Who Exactly?
1. **Title Level**: CEO? CTO? VP of Engineering? PMO Director? Chief Transformation Officer?
2. **Company Size**: Fortune 500? Series B-D startups? 100-500 employees? 
3. **Industry Focus**: All industries? Tech only? Traditional companies going digital?
4. **Pain Threshold**: What has to be happening for them to call you?
   - Just failed a digital transformation?
   - About to start an AI initiative?
   - Projects consistently over budget/timeline?
   - Board pressure for AI strategy?

### The Trigger Event
- What happens right before someone needs you?
- What headline makes your ideal client think "I need help"?
- What internal meeting makes them Google what you do?

### Disqualifiers
- What type of company/person would waste your time?
- What requests will you refer elsewhere?
- What budget level is too small?

### Your PM Tool as a Trojan Horse
- Do you give the tool away to get consulting deals?
- Do you charge for the tool separately?

- Could implementing your tool BE the consulting engagement?

### Pricing Psychology
- Are you pricing based on your time or their outcome?
- What's the ROI story? (If you save them $X million...)
- What's your walk-away point?

## üèÜ Credibility Architecture

### Leveraging Your PM Tool Story
1. **The Origin Story**
   - Why did you build it? (What problem made you angry enough to solve it?)
   - What did you sacrifice to build it?
   - What did you learn that changed your worldview?

2. **The Results Frame**
   - How many users has your tool had?
   - What's the total project value managed through it?
   - What efficiency gains have users reported?
   - Can you claim "I've overseen $X million in projects through my platform"?

3. **The Authority Transfer**
   - "I built what McKinsey would charge $2M to recommend"
   - "I've encoded best practices into software that runs 24/7"
   - "I don't just advise on project management - I've built the system that does it"

### Manufacturing Social Proof (Ethically)
- Can beta users of your tool become testimonial sources?
- Can you offer free strategic sessions to 5 dream clients in exchange for feedback?
- Will you do pro-bono work for a non-profit with a board full of executives?
- Can you turn your tool's insights into industry research reports?

## ü§î The Meta Question

**If your PM tool is so good at managing projects and providing insights, shouldn't your consulting practice be about implementing your own tool in organizations?**

Think about it:
- You're not selling consulting, you're selling transformation through technology
- You built the solution, now teach them to use it
- Your consulting is actually product implementation + change management
- You're a founder who consults, not a consultant who built something
